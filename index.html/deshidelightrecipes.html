1.üçóDeshi Chicken Kari (Deshi Style Chicken Curry).

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into medium pieces)
  • 3 tablespoons mustard oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 teaspoons ginger paste
  • 2 teaspoons garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (optional but adds richness)
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2‚Äì3 green chilies (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water (as needed)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a deep pan. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger and garlic paste. Saut√© for 1‚Äì2 minutes.
  3. Add chili, turmeric, cumin, and coriander powder. Stir well.
  4. Mix in yogurt and cook the masala until oil starts to separate.
  5. Add chicken pieces and salt. Cook on medium-high heat for 10‚Äì12 minutes, stirring often.
  6. Add water, cover, and simmer on low heat for 20‚Äì25 minutes until chicken is cooked through.
  7. Add green chilies and simmer for 5 more minutes for extra flavor.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or paratha.

2.ü•îAloo Bharta (Mashed Spiced Potatoes)

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium potatoes
  • 1‚Äì2 tablespoons mustard oil (or to taste)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2‚Äì3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (optional, chopped)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes until soft. Peel and mash them in a bowl.
  2. Add chopped onion, green chilies, and salt to the mashed potatoes.
  3. Drizzle mustard oil over the mash. (This gives it the authentic deshi flavor!)
  4. Mix everything well until smooth and evenly combined.
  5. Optionally, mix in chopped fresh coriander for added flavor.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature with rice and lentils (dal).

Ingredients:

  • 500g large prawns (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup onion paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 2‚Äì3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2‚Äì3 tablespoons mustard oil or ghee
  • 1 bay leaf & 2 cardamoms (optional for aroma)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate prawns with a little salt and turmeric. Set aside for 10‚Äì15 minutes.
  2. Heat mustard oil in a pan. Lightly fry the prawns for 1‚Äì2 minutes (do not overcook). Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same oil, add bay leaf and cardamoms. Then add onion paste and saut√© until golden.
  4. Add garlic and ginger paste, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Cook the masala till oil separates.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and stir well. Let it simmer for 4‚Äì5 minutes on low heat.
  6. Add the fried prawns and green chilies. Cover and cook for 5‚Äì7 more minutes.
  7. Turn off the heat once the gravy thickens and the prawns are tender.
  8. Serve hot with plain rice or pulao. Enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg mutton (preferably with bones)
  • 500g basmati rice or kalijeera rice
  • 1 cup plain yogurt (dahi)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and fried (beresta)
  • 1/2 cup mustard oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoons garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg & mace powder (jaifol-jayitri)
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom, cinnamon, and clove powder
  • 2‚Äì3 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup milk with saffron or food color (for layering)
  • Potatoes, peeled and halved (optional, traditional)

Instructions:

  1. Wash and drain the mutton. In a large bowl, marinate it with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, all spices, salt, and half of the fried onions. Cover and let it rest for 4‚Äì6 hours (or overnight for best flavor).
  2. Parboil the rice with whole garam masala, salt, and a little oil. Drain and keep aside.
  3. In a large heavy-bottomed pot (handi), grease the bottom with ghee or mustard oil.
  4. Layer the marinated mutton at the bottom. Add optional fried potatoes over it.
  5. Top with parboiled rice, then sprinkle saffron milk and the remaining fried onions.
  6. Seal the pot with foil or dough to trap the steam (‚Äúdum‚Äù method).
  7. Cook on low heat for 1.5 to 2 hours. First 10 minutes on medium flame, then reduce.
  8. Once done, gently mix before serving. Serve hot with salad and raita.

5.üçöBhuna Khichuri (Spiced Lentil Rice)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups aromatic rice (kalijeera or basmati)
  • 1 cup yellow lentils (mung dal), roasted
  • 500g beef or mutton (optional but traditional)
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and halved
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup onion (fried beresta)
  • 2 teaspoons ginger paste
  • 2 teaspoons garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 2‚Äì3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 bay leaves, 2 cardamoms, 2 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup mustard oil or ghee
  • 4 cups hot water (adjust as needed)

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak rice for 30 minutes. Roast mung dal dry until lightly golden, then wash and drain.
  2. In a large pan, heat oil or ghee. Add whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cinnamon, clove) and chopped onion. Fry until golden.
  3. Add garlic, ginger, turmeric, chili, and cumin powder. Saut√© well until oil separates.
  4. Add meat and salt. Cook until browned and tender. (Add water as needed.)
  5. Add potatoes and cook for 5‚Äì7 minutes with the masala.
  6. Add the washed rice and roasted lentils. Stir well with meat and spices.
  7. Pour hot water. Cover and cook on medium heat until rice and lentils are fully done and water is absorbed.
  8. Add fried onions (beresta) and green chilies. Mix gently.
  9. Cover and let it rest 5 more minutes before serving.

6.üëëShahi Payesh (Royal Bengali Rice Pudding)

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup aromatic rice (like gobindobhog or kalijeera)
  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4‚Äì5 cardamom pods
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (cashews, pistachios, almonds)
  • 1 tablespoon raisins
  • 1‚Äì2 tablespoons ghee
  • Few saffron strands (optional for royal touch)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice and soak it for 20 minutes. Drain well.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, boil the milk with cardamom and bay leaves. Stir occasionally.
  3. Once milk reduces slightly, add the rice and cook on low heat. Stir often to prevent sticking.
  4. When the rice is soft and the milk thickens, add sugar. Stir well and let it dissolve completely.
  5. In a small pan, heat ghee and lightly fry the nuts and raisins. Add them to the payesh.
  6. Add saffron if using. Simmer for a few more minutes, then turn off heat.
  7. Serve warm or chilled. Garnish with extra nuts before serving!
